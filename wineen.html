Our Wines

White Dry Wine "Phaedo"

Phaedon, in ancient times, meant "bright" and "luminous"—just like Phaedon wine from the Vassilaki Estate. A dry white table wine made from the finest clones of the Roditis grape variety, producing a wine with a radiant color, medium body, refreshing acidity, citrus aromas, and a pleasantly delicate flavor. The name "Roditis" comes from the reddish hue of the fruit, yet with proper vinification, it primarily yields white wine. The high-quality Roditis variety used by our family is cultivated in mountainous, arid vineyards, resulting in exceptional fruit. The berries are small, with thin, pinkish skin, while the flesh is soft and crisp. Germination begins in early April, and the grapes ripen around late September. It is beautifully paired with vegetables, Mediterranean stews, white meat, soft cheeses, and shellfish. Best servedat 8 to 12°C.

White Semi-Sweet Wine "Amelia"

Amelia means sweet and transparent. The Amelia from the Vassilaki Estate is a wine distinguished by its sweetness and pure clarity. Primarily a dessert white semi-sweet wine made from the finest clones of the Roditis grape variety, it boasts a transparent color, rich structure, balanced acidity, aromas of tropical fruits, and a clean aftertaste. It can also be enjoyed as an aperitif. The name of the Roditis variety comes from the reddish hue of the fruit. However, with proper vinification, it primarily produces white wine! Budding begins in early April, and the grapes ripen around late September. The fruit is small, with a thin rose-red skin, while its flesh is soft and crisp. The high-quality Roditis variety cultivated by our family is grown in mountainous and arid vineyards, resulting in exceptional fruit. It pairs beautifully with dried fruits, blue cheese, creamy sweets, and firm-textured fruits. Served at 6 to 10°C.

Red Semi-Sweet Wine "Anusia "

According to history, Anysia symbolizes perfection and completeness. It is a rosé semi-sweet table wine made from the ideal blend of selected Roditis and Agiorgitiko grape varieties. It can also be enjoyed as an aperitif. It is ideally paired with pasta, blue cheese, white meat, dried fruits, and desserts featuring red fruits. Served at 10 to 13°C.

Red Dry Wine "Leon"

The mighty Leon from the Vassilaki Estate is a wine with a bold personality. A dry red table wine made from the fine and traditional Agiorgitiko grape variety, it offers a deep red color, medium body and acidity, a velvety taste, and aromas of red fruits. The vine begins to sprout in late March and ripens by the end of September. The grape is medium-sized, ruby in color, with a thick skin and soft flesh. According to legend, its name of variety, originates from Saint George. As the story goes, a group of warriors stopped to rest in a vineyard before a battle and ate some of its fruit. The next day, they went into battle full of energy and joy. After their victory, they returned to the vineyard to celebrate—and to their great surprise, Saint George appeared before them. It pairs beautifully with red meats, grilled dishes, spicy foods, and hard cheeses. Best served at 14°C to 18°C.

Premium Red Dry Wine "Protogonos"

An exceptional dry red wine, aged in second- and third-use French oak barrels for optimal results. Mavrodaphne of Kefalonia

Mavrodaphne of Kefalonia is a grape variety known for its small-berried clone. The wine it produces is dark in color and full-bodied. Behind it lies a deeply romantic story. Gustav, the first to cultivate this variety back in 1854, fell madly in love with a stunning woman named Daphne, who had dark hair and eyes. Tragically, he lost her at a very young age. To honor her memory, he named the wine Mavrodaphne—literally meaning ‘’ black Daphne’’. Let yourself be enchanted by a bottle of Mavrodaphne from Vassilakis Estate, and indulge in its seductive aromas—plum, fig, coffee, and chocolate. It pairs beautifully with tiramisu, mocha mousse, or dark chocolate desserts.

White Dry Wine "Retsina"

Perhaps the most popular Greek wine. Its production dates back to antiquity, when the addition of resin was intended to help preserve the wine. Over time, it has evolved into a wine that holds a special place on international wine lists. Retsina from the Vassilaki Estate, made from a select Roditis grape variety and the ideal touch of pine resin, reveals its explosive character. Enjoy it with traditional Greek feta, stuffed vegetables, or moussaka, and experience the magic of Greek tradition! Best servedbetween 8°C and 12°C.

Robola

Robola is a dry white wine with a delicate persistent aroma, exceptional clarity and pronounced acidity.   It is grown on limestone and rocky soils and is perhaps the only variety that has given its name to the zone in which it is grown.  In the past, it was also called the “wine of the stone”.  Our region is the Robola zone where it is produced exclusively! Its fruit is yellowish, round with a thin skin.  The cultivation of Robola requires a lot of care and it’s quite demanding.   Our family’s vineyards are located at the foothills of Mt. Ainos where we produce the Vassilaki estate Robola with great love and care.  It is an ideal accompaniment to fish, seafood and shellfish, but in Kefalonia it is the inseparable companion of all meals!!!
